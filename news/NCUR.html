About NCUR

The National Conference on Undergraduate Research (NCUR) is the premier annual event celebrating undergraduate scholarship across all academic disciplines. Organized by the Council on Undergraduate Research, NCUR provides a platform for students from universities nationwide to present their original research, creative works, and scholarly achievements to a diverse audience of peers, faculty, and professionals.

This year's conference in Pittsburgh brought together thousands of undergraduate researchers, creating an inspiring environment for intellectual exchange and collaboration. The event featured presentations across multiple formats, including poster sessions, oral presentations, and performances, showcasing the breadth and depth of undergraduate research across the United States.

My Research Presentation

üîç My project, titled "Using Large Language Models to Enhance Emergency Response Systems: A Context Awareness Approach", explores how AI and Large Language Models like LLaMA 2 can revolutionize 911 emergency response systems by providing intelligent assistance to human dispatchers.

Research Overview

Classical emergency response systems face significant challenges in processing multiple emergency calls efficiently while maintaining high-quality responses. Human operators work under intense mental pressure, leading to potential errors in critical situations. My research proposes a comprehensive AI-powered system that supports human operators through advanced machine learning techniques.

Key Technical Innovations

  • Named Entity Recognition: Automatically extracting caller names and emergency addresses from voice calls using advanced NLP models, achieving 76.5% accuracy for address extraction and 48.3% for name extraction from real 911 call datasets.
  • Emergency Classification: Implementing a sophisticated classifier to detect emergency types (Medical, Crime, Fire, Mental Health, Hazardous Materials, etc.) using trained LLM models for accurate categorization and resource dispatch.
  • Modular LLM Architecture: Developing specialized Large Language Models for each emergency type using prompt engineering techniques and official emergency response manuals from public safety organizations.
  • Context-Aware Recommendations: Generating tailored, real-time guidance by integrating medical records, location data, and surrounding infrastructure information for personalized emergency response protocols.
  • Sentiment Analysis: Monitoring operator and caller stress levels to support responder mental health through intelligent break reminders and performance evaluation metrics.

Research Impact and Future Directions

The presentation at NCUR 2025 not only showcased my current research but also opened doors for future collaborations and research directions. Key areas for future exploration include:
