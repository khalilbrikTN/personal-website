Problem Statement

Peer assessment is valuable but often suffers from inconsistent or contradictory feedback. Conflicting reviews reduce student trust and create confusion, while instructor-led meta-reviews are time-consuming and difficult to scale. Our solution is an automated LLM-guided clustering method to group semantically similar feedback and highlight divergence.

Methodology

We utilized a multi-step clustering approach to align AI-generated clusters with human judgment.

  • Data: 15,500 peer reviews from a software development course.
  • Model: GPT-4 for semantic clustering and labeling.
  • Validation: Compared against human annotations using NMI, ARI, and F1-scores.
  • Explainability: GPT-based SHAP explanations vs. KeyBERT.

Key Results

Our approach demonstrated strong alignment with human feedback structures.

  • High Agreement: NMI of 0.816 and F1-score of 0.806.
  • Workload Reduction: Streamlined the meta-review process for instructors.
  • Persuasion: In 21% of cases, human annotators adopted the LLM's clustering entirely.

Technical Implementation

Four core technical components creating a comprehensive feedback analysis system.

  • Clustering: Grouping comments by semantic similarity.
  • Labeling: Generating concise, descriptive titles for clusters.
  • Disagreement Detection: Identifying conflicting viewpoints on the same artifact.
  • Keyword Extraction: Using GPT to explain why specific terms matter.
